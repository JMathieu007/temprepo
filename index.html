A bit of my story

I started in the military, where I learned quickly that no one is coming to save you. You either figure it out, or you get left behind. That mindset has shaped how I approach everything: work, learning, fitness, and the way I help other people.

After leaving active duty, I pushed hard into education and tech, moving into cybersecurity and cloud work. Along the way I realized something important: I don’t just want a job, I want to build things that actually matter and give me freedom.

These days I’m focused on combining all of that—discipline from the military, problem-solving from tech, and hard-earned life experience— into tools, systems, and content that help people get unstuck and move forward.

How I think

  • Ownership over excuses. If it’s my life, it’s my responsibility.
  • Simplify first. Most people don’t need more; they need less, done better.
  • Systems beat motivation. Build habits and infrastructure so progress is automatic.
  • Tell the truth. To myself, first. Then to everyone else.
